Fig. 1 — Positive Beam Composition vs Momentum
CERN T9 beamline positive secondary beam · measured fractions (%) of π⁺, p, K⁺ as function of beam momentum (GeV/c) · Source: CERN BL4S / HARP T9 documentation
Pion fraction drops from ~60% at low momentum to <6% above 10 GeV/c as proton contamination grows to ~90%. The dashed threshold marks the 60% purity target — only achievable post-separation at operating momenta of 10–15 GeV/c.
Fig. 2 — π⁺ Yield vs Target Atomic Mass (A)
Integrated π⁺ yield from 12 GeV/c proton beam on different targets · HARP experiment, CERN PS · Catanesi et al. Eur. Phys. J. C (2008)
Yield scales as A^0.70. Pb (A=208) produces ~2.8× the yield of Al (A=27).
Fig. 3 — π⁺ Production Cross-Section vs Proton Momentum
Integrated π⁺ cross-section (mb) for p+C at different incident momenta · HARP, Phys. Rev. C 77, 055207 (2008)
Cross-section at 12–15 GeV/c is ~3.1× the value at 3 GeV/c. Shaded band: combined statistical + systematic uncertainty.
Fig. 4 — π⁺ Momentum Distribution Post-Collision
Momentum distribution of π⁺ produced by 15 GeV/c p+Pb · Gaussian fit to HARP forward spectrometer data · σ ≈ 1.4 GeV/c, μ ≈ 10.8 GeV/c
μ = 10.8 GeV/c  |  σ = 1.4 GeV/c  |  FWHM ≈ 3.3 GeV/c
The beam selection window (10–12 GeV/c) sits within 1σ of the peak. Green fill shows the selected fraction.
Fig. 5 — Lorentz Factor γ & Dilated Lifetime vs Momentum
Relativistic time dilation for π⁺ (m = 139.6 MeV/c²) · τ₀ = 26.03 ns · τ_lab = γ·τ₀
At 15 GeV/c: γ ≈ 107  |  τ_lab ≈ 2.79 μs  |  Mean free path ≈ 837 m
Fig. 6 — Magnetic Deflection Angle vs Momentum @ 15 GeV/c
θ = 0.3·B·L/p (mrad) for B = 1.5 T, L = 1 m dipole · singly-charged particles · separation arises from post-collision momentum asymmetry
All +1 particles deflect identically at the same momentum. The angular separation between π⁺ (peak 10.8 GeV/c) and p (peak 13.0 GeV/c) is ~4.2 mrad — sufficient for downstream collimation.
Fig. 7 — π⁺ Yield vs Target Thickness (λ_int)
Relative π⁺ yield vs target thickness for Pb, Cu, Al · 12 GeV/c proton beam · HARP long-target data (2008)
Yield saturates beyond ~0.5λ as secondary absorption cancels further production. Optimal range 0.2–0.4λ (shaded) for all three materials.
Fig. 8 — Beam Composition: Before vs After Separation
Simulated beam composition at 15 GeV/c · Before (primary beam) vs After (post dipole + lead absorber) · HARP cross-section ratios
Before: 92% p · 6% π⁺ · 2% K⁺  |  After: 62% π⁺ · 32% p · 6% K⁺
Fig. 9 — β vs Momentum (Cherenkov PID)
β = p/√(p²+m²) · Used by Cherenkov detectors to distinguish π⁺, K⁺, p at equal momentum
At 15 GeV/c: β(π⁺) = 0.99997  |  β(K⁺) = 0.99979  |  β(p) = 0.99817
π–p separation: Δβ = 0.00180 — resolvable in high-pressure gas Cherenkov systems (CEDAR/DISC).
Fig. 10 — Double-Differential π⁺ Cross-Section vs Secondary Momentum (HARP)
d²σ/dpdΩ (mb/GeV/c·sr) for 12 GeV/c p+C, p+Cu, p+Pb at θ = 50 mrad · HARP, CERN PS, Phys. Rev. C 77, 055207 (2008) · forward spectrometer
Cross-section peaks at 1–2 GeV/c secondary momentum and falls as 1/p² at higher values. A-scaling (~A^0.7) holds across all three targets. At 10–12 GeV/c, Pb gives σ ≈ 0.6 mb/(GeV/c·sr). Error bars: combined statistical + systematic (~5–10%).
